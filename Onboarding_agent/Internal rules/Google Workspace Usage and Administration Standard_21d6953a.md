Google Workspace Usage and Administration Standard

Purpose
Define how employees use Google Workspace (Gmail, Drive, Calendar, Meet, Chat, and related services) and how administrators manage access, security, and data protection.

Scope
All users with a company Google Workspace account, including employees, contractors, and service accounts.

Core principles
Use company Google Workspace accounts for all business communication and document collaboration.
Protect company data using built-in security features such as two-step verification and DLP rules.
Follow data classification rules when sharing, syncing, or exporting content.

Account usage
Use your company account (e.g., first.last@example.invalid) for business email, calendars, and shared drives.
Do not forward all company email to personal accounts.
Personal Gmail accounts must not be used to store Confidential or Restricted company data.

Passwords and authentication
Two-step verification (2SV) is mandatory for all accounts.
Use only approved authentication methods (e.g., security keys, authenticator app) as configured by IT.
Do not share passwords or 2SV codes; IT will never ask for them.

Gmail guidelines
Use labels and filters to organize email instead of creating local PST/mbox archives.
Do not send Confidential data unencrypted to external recipients unless business justified and approved.
Report suspicious messages using the built-in phishing or spam report options.

Google Drive and Shared Drives
Store team documents in Shared Drives rather than My Drive when they are needed by multiple people.
Use "Viewer", "Commenter", and "Editor" roles to enforce least privilege.
Do not grant "Anyone with the link" access for Confidential or Restricted data.
Remove access for external collaborators when they no longer need it.

Document sharing rules
Prefer sharing with groups (e.g., team groups) instead of individuals for easier onboarding and offboarding.
Review existing sharing settings regularly for critical team folders.
When sharing outside the company, use the minimum necessary access and time-bound sharing when possible.

Calendar and Meet
Keep work calendars up to date with availability, including focus time and time off.
Do not add sensitive incident details or personal medical information to calendar event descriptions.
Use company Meet links for business meetings; avoid recording unless there is a clear business need.

Chat and spaces
Use company Chat and spaces for day-to-day collaboration instead of external consumer messaging apps.
Do not share Confidential or Restricted data in large public spaces without a documented purpose and moderator.
Respect conduct guidelines in chat messages and reactions.

Data loss prevention and retention
Data Loss Prevention (DLP) rules may automatically warn, block, or quarantine sharing actions that involve sensitive patterns such as payment card numbers or personal identifiers.
Retention rules in Google Workspace may archive or delete messages and files after defined periods; do not attempt to bypass retention by moving data to personal systems.

Onboarding and offboarding
New users are provisioned with a standard Workspace license, baseline groups, and Shared Drive access based on role.
When an employee leaves, their account is suspended, ownership of critical Drive content is transferred, and mailbox handling is configured per HR/manager instructions.

Requests and support
Use the IT service portal to request:
New Google Groups or changes to membership
New Shared Drives or permission changes
Delegated access to mailboxes or calendars
Contact the Google Workspace Administrator team via gw-admins@example.invalid for issues that block work.

Violations and monitoring
Workspace usage may be monitored for security, compliance, and operational reasons in line with company acceptable use and privacy notices. Violations of this standard may result in access changes or disciplinary action.
