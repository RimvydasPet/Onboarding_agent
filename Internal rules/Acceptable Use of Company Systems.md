# Acceptable Use of Company Systems

## Purpose
This policy defines acceptable and prohibited use of company-owned or company-managed devices, accounts, networks, and software.

## Scope
Applies to all employees, contractors, interns, and third parties with access to company systems.

## Key principles
- Company systems are provided primarily for business purposes.
- Security and privacy must be balanced; monitoring may occur to protect the company.
- Least privilege and need-to-know access are standard.

## Allowed use
- Business communication and collaboration using approved tools.
- Limited personal use that is incidental, lawful, and does not interfere with work.
- Installing software only via approved methods (managed app catalog or IT-approved request).

## Prohibited use
- Sharing passwords, MFA devices, or access tokens.
- Disabling security controls (EDR/AV, disk encryption, firewall, MDM).
- Using unapproved storage or file-sharing for company data (e.g., personal cloud drives).
- Attempting to access systems or data not explicitly authorized.
- Any activity that violates law, contractual obligations, or company conduct rules.

## Data handling rules (high level)
- Use company-approved storage for internal documents.
- Do not copy confidential content to personal email or messaging apps.
- Encrypt sensitive files when sharing externally, and use expiration links when available.

## Monitoring and logging
The company may log device and network activity to detect threats, ensure compliance, and troubleshoot incidents. Monitoring is focused on system security and reliability, not personal profiling.

## Violations and consequences
Violations may lead to access revocation, disciplinary action up to termination, and legal action if applicable.

## Exceptions
Exceptions require written approval from Head of IT and the Security Officer, with an expiration date and compensating controls.
